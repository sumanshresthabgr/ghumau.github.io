Lotte World

Lotte World is a major recreation complex located in the heart of Seoul, South Korea, known for being home to the world's largest indoor theme park, Lotte World Adventure.

Opened in 1989, this entertainment colossus offers a blend of thrilling rides, parades, and attractions that operate year-round, regardless of the weather. It also includes a four-season indoor ice rink and is connected to the imposing Lotte World Tower, one of the world's tallest buildings, which offers an observation deck with panoramic views of Seoul.

Lotte World attracts millions of visitors annually and is a popular spot for both local and international tourists seeking a diverse range of entertainment and sightseeing options in one convenient location.

Different locations Of lotte World

1. Lotte World Adventure (Seoul) The flagship location in Jamsil, Seoul, is home to the world’s largest indoor theme park and the outdoor Magic Island. It features classic adventurous rides, character parades, seasonal festivals, an ice rink, a folk museum, luxury hotels, and seamless access to Lotte World Tower—one of the tallest buildings in the world.

2. Lotte World Tower & Seoul Sky (Seoul) Connected to the Seoul park, the 123-story Lotte World Tower includes an art gallery, luxury shopping, a cinema complex, restaurants, hotels, and the breathtaking Seoul Sky Observatory. It symbolizes modern Seoul and has become one of Korea’s most recognized landmarks.

3. Lotte World Adventure Busan (Busan) Opened in 2022 in Busan, this is Korea’s newest major theme park. Designed like a European fantasy village, it blends classic storybook themes with modern rides. Highlights include: Giant Digger (Busan’s most intense roller coaster) Giant Swing, Giant Splash, and multiple immersive zones Large outdoor plazas perfect for parades and seasonal events This Busan location provides a completely outdoor park atmosphere with ocean-inspired designs, making it a major attraction for tourists visiting southern Korea.

4. Lotte Water Park (Gimhae, near Busan) One of the biggest water parks in the country, featuring: Massive indoor and outdoor wave pools Korea’s first “Bumerango” water slide Polynesian-themed relaxation zones Family pools and children’s water playgrounds This park is especially popular in summer and is one of the top water destinations in Korea.

5. Lotte World Aquarium (Seoul) Located next to the Lotte World Tower, this is one of Korea’s largest aquariums, home to over 55,000 marine animals. It focuses on nature conservation, educational exhibits, underwater tunnels, and unique species like belugas and penguins.

6. Lotte World Aquarium (Busan – Shinsegae area) Although not branded as “Lotte” in the same way, Busan is also home to large marine attractions managed by the Lotte network through local partnerships. These include ocean ecology zones, sea tunnels, and family-friendly educational experiences.

7. Lotte Resorts (Jeju, Sokcho, and Buyeo) Lotte Group operates large resort complexes that, while not strictly “Lotte World Adventure,” offer Lotte’s signature entertainment facilities. Notable locations include: Lotte Resort Jeju Art Villas Lotte Resort Sokcho (with ocean views and water facilities) Lotte Resort Buyeo (connected to cultural attractions) These resorts include water parks, spas, luxury hotel services, and adventure-themed activities.

8. Lotte Department Stores, Malls, Cinemas (Nationwide) Lotte World’s entertainment vision extends into many cities through: Lotte Malls (Seoul, Incheon, Suwon, Daegu, Busan) Lotte Cinemas Lotte Duty Free stores Many of these locations include indoor play zones, VR experiences, children’s rides, and event spaces similar in style to Lotte World.
